                 There is an old foliation (of cent.
                     xiv ) first clearly visible on f. 2r (p.  3 )
                    where it stands as 46. 44 leaves of text have therefore disappeared. The edges
                    of two old leaves survive at the beginning. They carry a large double leaf of
                    vellum ruled for double columns: now folded. On f.  2r  is a Parkerian table of contents. The pagination which I use
                    here begins after this. A strip of vellum has been pasted over the top of f. 1r
                    (p.  1 ),  now lifted and foliated as
                         iiir  . The title  Homiliaram Saxonicarum Lib.
                            9 us   is written on this. See  Wanley, p. 133

                        Aelfric's Lives (II, p. xiii) . He uses it for the Homilies on Ash
                    Wednesday, Mid Lent, All Saints, Auguries, Absalom and Achitophel.  Liebermann, Gesetze, calls it Cx and
                        uses it for the excommunication form (p. 438) . See  Anglia VI 446 .
