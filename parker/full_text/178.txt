                 On the flyleaf  (f.  iir ) 
                    is a list ( xii ? ) of the Homilies, in Latin,  in a
                        hand which has glossed both volumes. It is the same tremulous hand which
                        occurs in  MS 12  and  MS 198  . Its presence shows that the two
                    portions of this MS. have been long bound together, and also that they belonged
                    anciently to  Worcester Priory . See on  MS 12 . 
                 The list of contents  (f.
                         iir )  begins: I. Exameron and ends xxvii De
                    septiformi spiritu.  On the verso  (f.
                             iiv )  this same hand has written a number of
                        Anglo-Saxon words with their Latin equivalents . Many are lined
                    through. 
                 A paper flyleaf of  Parker 's time,
                    with a list of contents, follows  (f.  iiiv ) ,
                    headed: Secundus liber.

                    Exameron, erased. There is a trace of a mark at the R. upper corner.
