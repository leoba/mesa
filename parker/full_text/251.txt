                 The book is doubtless from  Bury St
                        Edmunds . On f.  1r  is the pressmark E. 43. It is not easy to see why this pressmark was given to this
                        book. The letters of the pressmarks of  Bury 
                        uniformly corresponded to the subjects of the books (e.g. B for Biblia):
                        also, there is another extant volume marked E. 43 (viz.  Lambeth MS. 218 , Epistolae Alcuini). The proper
                        letter for the present volume is C (=Chronica).  A long life of  St Edmund  is in the text, and there are notes relating
                    to the Abbey at the end. 


                 The  Cronica que liber Regius appellatur  is referred to
                    in Flete's  History of Westminster Abbey  (see  MS 287. 3 ) See the
                        edition by  Dr J. Armitage
                            Robinson, Dean of Westminster, 1909, p. 3 .  (but the passage
                    cited by him is not in the text of this MS.) with the note  Cronica Regia ideo
                        dicitur quia  Rex Richardus secundus  eam scribi
                        fecit a monacho quodam cenobii  S. Edmundi de
                        Burye .  I think it possible that the present MS. contains a
                    shortened text of this  Chronica Regia .
