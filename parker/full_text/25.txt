                 On flyleaf  (f.  ir )

                 At the end of the table of contents  (f.
                             iiv )  is an erasure which, revived, looks like
                         liber Abbendonie  (i.e.  Abingdon Abbey ). 

                 
                     The ornament of the book is good. The first Epistle has an initial
                             (f.  1r )  of  Cyprian  in green mitre and lilac chasuble (with pattern of
                        groups of 3 gold dots) seated with crosier blessing: gold ground. The other
                        tracts have blue initials with elaborate interiors in red leaving designs in
                        white. 
                 

                 On second flyleaf  (f.  iiv ) :  In hoc
                        uolumine continentur epistole  beati Cypriani 
                        episcopi cartaginensis ex dictis  Sancti Augustini 
                        (lined through) magni oratoris et gloriosi martiris.  Followed by list of
                    74 epistles. 

                 The order of contents corresponds
                    exactly with that in  Pembroke College 154 
                    (cent.  xii , from  Buildwas ); but the
                    latter adds  Ymnus de pascha . See on the MSS. of  Cyprian 
                     Hans v. Soden in  Texte u. Untersuchungen  N. F. x.  A collation of this MS. was
                    supplied to  Fell and Pearson for
                        their edition (1682) .
